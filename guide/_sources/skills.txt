Essential Skills List
=====================

The following lab skills are really *life skills* and students will need to
learn as many as possible to be effective in a prototyping laboratory
environment.  It may also be useful to refer to the :ref:`topic-guides` section
and `the essential skills posts on the F14 course web site`_.

Following each recommended skill is a very rough list of links to tutorials;
probably many will be obsolete by the time you read this.

CAD/CAM Resources
=================

**Online repositories of shared 3d models:**

-  `Thingiverse <http://www.thingiverse.com/>`__: "MakerBot's
   Thingiverse is a thriving design community for discovering, making,
   and sharing 3D printable things. "
-  `SketchFab <https://sketchfab.com/>`__: "Sketchfab is the leading
   platform to publish, share and embed interactive 3D content, anywhere
   online. "
-  `Google 3d Warehouse <https://3dwarehouse.sketchup.com/>`__: The
   Google company's database of 3d models
-  `3D Content Central <http://www.3dcontentcentral.com/>`__ Free 2D and
   3D CAD Models run by the makers of SolidWorks

**Online tools for CAD/CAM:**

-  `BoxMaker <http://http://boxmaker.connectionlab.org/>`__: designing laser cut finger
   joint boxes made easy
-  `AutoDesk 123 Make <http://www.123dapp.com/make>`__: "3D meets DIY.
    Turn your amazing 3D models into even more amazing do-it-yourself
   projects. "
-  `GearMaker <https://woodgears.ca/gear_cutting/template.html>`__:
   Online tool for designing Gears
-  `Involute Spur Gear
   Builder <http://hessmer.org/gears/InvoluteSpurGearBuilder.html>`__:
   can generate DXF files to import into SolidWorks or Rhino

**References for designers:**

-  `CNC Joinery <http://artfab.art.cmu.edu/cnc-joinery/>`__: Read all
   about it
-  `Curved/Bending hard materials with Laser
   Cutter <http://www.instructables.com/id/Curved-laser-bent-wood/>`__:
   Read all about it
-  `Google Books: 507 Mechanical
   Movements <http://books.google.com/books?id=vOhIAAAAMAAJ&dq=mechanical%20movements&pg=PR1#v=onepage&q&f=false>`__
-  `Google Books: The Engineer's Sketch-book of Mechanical Movements,
   Devices, Appliances, Contrivances, and
   Details <http://books.google.com/books?id=LlYSAAAAIAAJ&dq=mechanical%20movements&pg=PP1#v=onepage&q&f=false>`__

**Awesome Little Free (or almost free) Programs:**

-  `Pepakura <http://www.tamasoft.co.jp/pepakura-en/>`__: Origami tool;  unfold complex 3d objects and cut out of flat material
-  `Meshlab <http://meshlab.sourceforge.net/>`__:  Opensource/Multiplatform mesh manipulator
-  `SketchUp <http://www.sketchup.com/>`__: "The easiest way to draw in 3D"
-  `Grasshopper <http://grasshopper.com/>`__: Parametric design for  Rhino
